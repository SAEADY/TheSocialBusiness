Fake news, rumors, and wrong information are big problems for all types of media, but especially on social media. It's easy for false information to spread quickly, and it's hard to correct it once it's out there. But social media isn't all bad. It's also been important for spreading important messages, like during the #BlackLivesMatter movement or the #MeToo movement. Still, many argue that social media does not have enough regulations in place to prevent false news from spreading especially as it becomes more and more people's primary source for news. Over 80% of Americans now get news from digital devices, surpassing TV, radio, and print. Among young adults, social media is the top news source. Even 53% of all Americans rely on it for news. Platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and TikTok act as news hubs, blurring opinions and facts.

MIT researchers discovered that fake news travels up to 10 times faster than real news on social media. What we encounter on social platforms is created by algorithms whose goal is to keep us engaged. The longer we stay online, the more personalized ads these platforms can sell to us. This business model drives major platforms, focusing on maximizing time spent on the platform and ad revenue.

This has led to many real world consequences. Such as rapid and changing misinformation spread during Covid-19 where being confined at home led more and more people to accept social media news as their primary source. Often this misinformation is used to promote certain beliefs or political motives, such as in the case of what is now refered to as "#PizzaGate" a conspiracy theory and spread of false information intended to discredit Hillary Clinton. A social media rumour spread that Hillary Clinton and her staff were involved in various illegal activities which through more false articles then spiralled into the misinformation that she was running a child trafficking ring out of a pizza place in Washington. This misinformation led a North-Carolina man to visit the pizza place with a gun and to fire his weapon (not hitting anyone) in a very misguided attempt to rescue children.
