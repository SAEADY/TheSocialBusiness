Social Strain

Social media has also redefined the way we can make connections and keep in touch with each other. It's online communities can provide a space for sharing that people might not have access to otherwose in their lives. During COVID-19, social interaction and humor on social media eased stress. However overuse can lead to multiple problems, studies find correlation between mental health and social media and suggest it may lead to body image issues, addiction, cyberbullying, and worsening moods. Excessive use can worsen these feelings of loneliness, fear of missing out, and lower life satisfaction.

Between 2012 and 2015, depression increased by 21% in boys and 50% in girls , with social media, particularly excessive use, playing a significant role. While many factors play a part in that total number, studies indicate that heavy social media usage adversely affects self-esteem and sleep patterns, particularly among young people, while older adults are less affected. More than 1/3 (38%) of adults perceive social media as harmful, despite its perceived benefits.
